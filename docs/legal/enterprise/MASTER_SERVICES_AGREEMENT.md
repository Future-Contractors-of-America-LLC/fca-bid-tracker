# Master Services Agreement

**Future Contractors of America LLC**  
**Template Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** June 19, 2026  

> **Prepared for legal review — not legal advice.** Execute with Order Form for enterprise customers.

---

## PARTIES

This Master Services Agreement ("**Agreement**") is entered into as of the Effective Date in the Order Form by and between:

**Provider:** Future Contractors of America LLC, a Virginia limited liability company ("**FCA**")

**Customer:** The entity identified in the Order Form ("**Customer**")

FCA and Customer are each a "**Party**" and collectively the "**Parties**."

---

## 1. ORDER FORMS AND STRUCTURE

### 1.1 Order Forms

Each Order Form referencing this Agreement describes Services, fees, term, user limits, support tier, and any deviations. Order Forms are incorporated by reference. Conflict order: Order Form ? this Agreement ? Exhibits ? Documentation.

### 1.2 Affiliates

Customer Affiliates may use the Services under Customer's account if Customer remains responsible for their compliance and payment.

---

## 2. SERVICES

### 2.1 Provision

FCA will provide the cloud software services, support, and professional services (if any) described in the Order Form ("**Services**") in accordance with this Agreement, the [Service Level Agreement](./SERVICE_LEVEL_AGREEMENT.md) (Exhibit A, if applicable), and the [Security Addendum](./SECURITY_ADDENDUM.md) (Exhibit B).

### 2.2 Documentation

"**Documentation**" means FCA's then-current user guides and API documentation for the subscribed Services.

### 2.3 Changes

FCA may update the Services for improvement, security, or compliance. Material reductions to subscribed core functionality during a paid term require notice and a remedy per the SLA or pro-rata credit.

---

## 3. CUSTOMER RESPONSIBILITIES

Customer will: (a) provide accurate account information; (b) maintain credential security; (c) ensure users comply with the [Terms of Service](./TERMS_OF_SERVICE.md) and [Acceptable Use Policy](./ACCEPTABLE_USE_POLICY.md); (d) obtain consents for personal data submitted to the Services; (e) maintain appropriate backups of Customer Data where required beyond FCA's standard retention; (f) comply with export and sanctions laws.

---

## 4. FEES AND PAYMENT

### 4.1 Fees

Customer will pay fees in the Order Form. Unless stated otherwise, fees are annual in advance, non-cancelable, and non-refundable except as expressly provided.

### 4.2 Invoicing

Invoices are due net thirty (30) days from invoice date. Late amounts accrue interest at 1.5% per month or the maximum allowed by law.

### 4.3 Taxes

Customer pays applicable taxes excluding taxes on FCA's net income. Customer provides valid tax exemption certificates where applicable.

### 4.4 Audit Rights (Usage)

If the Order Form is seat- or usage-based, FCA may audit usage annually with reasonable notice. Underpayment is invoiced at list price.

---

## 5. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 5.1 Term

Initial term and renewals are stated in the Order Form. Unless auto-renewal is disabled per notice, subscriptions renew for successive periods equal to the initial term.

### 5.2 Termination for Convenience

Either Party may terminate at end of a term with written notice per the Order Form notice period (default: sixty (60) days before renewal).

### 5.3 Termination for Cause

Either Party may terminate for material breach not cured within thirty (30) days of written notice. FCA may suspend immediately for non-payment, security threats, or AUP violations.

### 5.4 Effect of Termination

Upon termination, Customer's access ends. Customer may export Customer Data for thirty (30) days. FCA will delete Customer Data per the DPA and retention schedule unless law requires retention.

---

## 6. CONFIDENTIALITY

### 6.1 Definition

"**Confidential Information**" means non-public information disclosed by a Party marked confidential or reasonably understood to be confidential, including pricing, security information, and Customer Data.

### 6.2 Obligations

Recipient will: (a) use Confidential Information only to perform under this Agreement; (b) protect it with at least the same care as its own (not less than reasonable care); (c) disclose only to need-to-know personnel and contractors bound by confidentiality.

### 6.3 Exclusions

Confidential Information does not include information that is public without breach, independently developed, rightfully received without restriction, or required to be disclosed by law (with notice where permitted).

### 6.4 Duration

Confidentiality survives five (5) years after termination; trade secrets survive as long as protected.

---

## 7. DATA PROTECTION

### 7.1 DPA

Where FCA processes personal data on Customer's behalf, the [Data Processing Agreement](./DATA_PROCESSING_AGREEMENT.md) (Exhibit C) applies and is incorporated by reference.

### 7.2 Security

FCA maintains safeguards described in Exhibit B (Security Addendum).

### 7.3 Subprocessors

FCA uses subprocessors listed at https://futurecontractorsofamerica.com/subprocessors and will provide notice of material changes per the DPA.

## 8. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### 8.1 FCA IP

FCA retains all rights in the Services, software, trademarks, documentation, and derivatives. No rights granted except as expressly stated.

### 8.2 Customer Data

Customer retains all rights in Customer Data. Customer grants FCA a license to process Customer Data to provide the Services.

### 8.3 Feedback

Customer grants FCA a royalty-free license to use feedback without restriction.

### 9.1 Mutual

Each Party warrants it has authority to enter this Agreement.

### 9.2 Services

FCA warrants the Services will materially conform to Documentation during the subscription term. Exclusive remedy: re-performance or termination and pro-rata refund for the affected period, or SLA credits.

### 9.3 Disclaimer

EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY STATED, SERVICES ARE PROVIDED "AS IS." FCA DISCLAIMS IMPLIED WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY, FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE, AND NON-INFRINGEMENT.

---

## 10. INDEMNIFICATION

### 10.1 By FCA

FCA will defend Customer against third-party claims that the Services infringe a U.S. patent, copyright, or trademark, and pay resulting damages and costs, if Customer: (a) notifies FCA promptly; (b) gives FCA sole control of defense; (c) cooperates reasonably. FCA has no obligation for claims arising from Customer Data, modifications, combinations with non-FCA products, or use after notice to discontinue. If infringement is likely, FCA may modify, replace, or terminate affected Services with pro-rata refund.

### 10.2 By Customer

Customer will defend FCA against claims arising from Customer Data, Customer's products/services, or Customer's breach of this Agreement or law.

---

## 11. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

### 11.1 Exclusion

NEITHER PARTY IS LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, OR LOST PROFITS, REVENUE, OR DATA.

### 11.2 Cap

EXCEPT FOR EXCLUDED CLAIMS, EACH PARTY'S AGGREGATE LIABILITY WILL NOT EXCEED FEES PAID OR PAYABLE BY CUSTOMER IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM.

### 11.3 Excluded Claims

Caps do not apply to: payment obligations; breach of confidentiality; indemnification obligations; fraud or willful misconduct; or liabilities that cannot be limited by law.

---

## 12. INSURANCE

FCA will maintain during the term: (a) commercial general liability ? $1M per occurrence; (b) cyber liability ? $1M per occurrence (or commercially reasonable limits); (c) workers' compensation as required by law. Certificates provided upon reasonable request.

---

## 13. COMPLIANCE

Each Party will comply with applicable laws, including anti-bribery (FCPA, UK Bribery Act), export controls, and data protection. Customer will not use the Services in violation of the [Export Compliance Policy](./EXPORT_COMPLIANCE_AND_SANCTIONS_POLICY.md).

---

## 14. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 14.1 Governing Law

Commonwealth of **Virginia**, excluding conflict-of-law rules.

### 14.2 Escalation

Disputes escalate: operational contacts ? executive sponsors ? mediation in Virginia within thirty (30) days of written notice.

### 14.3 Venue

Exclusive jurisdiction in state or federal courts in Virginia. Either Party may seek injunctive relief for IP or confidentiality breaches.

### 14.4 Optional Arbitration

If the Order Form selects arbitration, disputes are resolved by AAA Commercial Rules, one arbitrator, Virginia, English language.
