---

## 1. PARTIES AND ROLES

This Data Processing Agreement ("**DPA**") forms part of the agreement between:

- **Customer** ( "**Controller**" or "**Business**" where CCPA applies)
- **Future Contractors of America LLC** ("**FCA**" or "**Processor**" / "**Service Provider**")

This DPA applies when FCA processes Personal Data on behalf of Customer in providing the Services.

---

## 2. DEFINITIONS

- **"Applicable Data Protection Law"** means GDPR, UK GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, and other laws governing Personal Data processing under this DPA.
- **"Personal Data"** means information relating to an identified or identifiable individual processed by FCA on Customer's behalf.
- **"Subprocessor"** means a third party engaged by FCA to process Personal Data.
- Capitalized terms not defined here have meanings in the MSA or Applicable Data Protection Law.

---

## 3. SCOPE AND INSTRUCTIONS

### 3.1 Processing

FCA will process Personal Data only: (a) to provide the Services per the MSA; (b) as documented in Annex I; (c) per Customer's documented instructions unless required by law (in which case FCA notifies Customer unless prohibited).

### 3.2 Customer Instructions

Customer instructs FCA to perform processing described in Annex I. Additional instructions via support tickets or admin configuration must be compatible with the Services.

### 3.3 Controller Responsibility

Customer is responsible for: lawful basis for processing; notices to data subjects; accuracy of Personal Data; and not submitting prohibited special-category data without lawful basis and safeguards.

---

## 4. FCA OBLIGATIONS

FCA will:

1. Process Personal Data only on documented instructions
2. Ensure personnel are bound by confidentiality
3. Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures per Annex II and the [Security Addendum](./SECURITY_ADDENDUM.md)
4. Engage Subprocessors per Section 6
5. Assist with data subject requests per Section 8
6. Assist with DPIAs and consultations where required
7. Notify Customer of Personal Data breaches per Section 9
8. Delete or return Personal Data per Section 10
9. Make available information necessary to demonstrate compliance and allow audits per Section 11
10. Inform Customer if an instruction infringes Applicable Data Protection Law

---

## 5. DETAILS OF PROCESSING — ANNEX I

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Subject matter** | Provision of FCA cloud contractor platform Services |
| **Duration** | Term of MSA + export/deletion period |
| **Nature and purpose** | Hosting, storage, computation, display, backup, support, security monitoring |
| **Categories of data subjects** | Customer employees, contractors, portal users, project contacts |

## 6. SUBPROCESSORS

### 6.1 Authorization

Customer authorizes FCA to engage Subprocessors listed at https://futurecontractorsofamerica.com/subprocessors.

### 6.2 Notice

FCA will notify Customer of new or replacement Subprocessors at least thirty (30) days in advance. Customer may object on reasonable data-protection grounds within fourteen (14) days. If unresolved, Customer may terminate affected Services with pro-rata refund.

### 6.3 Flow-Down

FCA imposes data protection obligations on Subprocessors substantially similar to this DPA.

---

## 7. INTERNATIONAL TRANSFERS

### 7.1 Transfer Mechanism

For transfers from EEA/UK/Switzerland to countries without adequacy decisions, the Parties agree to the **EU Standard Contractual Clauses (2021/914)** Module Two (Controller to Processor) or Module Three (Processor to Processor), as applicable, incorporated by reference.

### 7.2 UK Addendum

UK transfers use the UK International Data Transfer Addendum to the EU SCCs (ICO version) where required.

### 7.3 Supplementary Measures

Additional measures are described in the [Data Residency and Transfers Policy](./DATA_RESIDENCY_AND_TRANSFERS_POLICY.md).

---

## 8. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS

FCA will, to the extent legally permitted, assist Customer in responding to data subject requests (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection) by providing self-service tools or reasonable support. Customer is responsible for responses to data subjects.

**Contact for assistance:** privacy@futurecontractorsofamerica.com

---

## 9. SECURITY BREACHES

FCA will notify Customer without undue delay (and within seventy-two (72) hours where GDPR applies) after confirming a Personal Data breach affecting Customer Personal Data. Notification includes: nature of breach, categories and approximate numbers of data subjects and records, likely consequences, and measures taken or proposed.

FCA will cooperate with Customer's regulatory notification obligations.

Full procedures: [Incident Response and Breach Notification Policy](./INCIDENT_RESPONSE_AND_BREACH_NOTIFICATION_POLICY.md)

---

## 10. RETURN AND DELETION

Upon termination or Customer request, FCA will delete or return Personal Data within thirty (30) days unless retention is required by law. Deletion from backups occurs on rolling schedule (typically within ninety (90) days).

---

## 11. AUDITS AND CERTIFICATIONS

### 11.1 Information

FCA will provide security documentation, completed questionnaires (within reason), and SOC 2 report when available under NDA.

### 11.2 Audits

Customer may audit no more than once annually with thirty (30) days' notice, during business hours, without disrupting operations. Customer bears audit costs unless material non-compliance is found.

---

## 12. CCPA / CPRA (SERVICE PROVIDER TERMS)

Where CCPA applies, FCA:

- Processes Personal Data as a **Service Provider** / **Processor** for business purposes specified in the MSA
- Will not **sell** or **share** Personal Data
- Will not retain, use, or disclose Personal Data outside the business relationship except as permitted by CPRA
- Certifies understanding of restrictions and will notify Customer if it can no longer meet obligations

Customer has the right to take reasonable steps to ensure FCA uses Personal Data consistently with Customer's obligations.

---

## 13. LIABILITY

DPA liability is subject to the limitation of liability in the MSA unless Applicable Data Protection Law requires otherwise.

---

## 14. TERM

This DPA remains in effect for the duration of processing and until all Personal Data is deleted or returned.
