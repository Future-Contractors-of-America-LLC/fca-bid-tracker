## PARTIES

- **Covered Entity / Business Associate:** [Customer legal name] ("**Covered Entity**")

---

## 1. BACKGROUND

Covered Entity may disclose PHI to FCA solely to perform services under the MSA/Order Form. This Agreement satisfies HIPAA Business Associate requirements at 45 CFR §§ 164.502(e) and 164.504(e).

---

## 2. DEFINITIONS

Terms have meanings in HIPAA Rules (45 CFR Parts 160 and 164). **Breach**, **Protected Health Information (PHI)**, **Security Incident**, and **Subcontractor** have HIPAA meanings.

---

## 3. OBLIGATIONS OF BUSINESS ASSOCIATE

FCA will:

1. Not use or disclose PHI other than as permitted by this Agreement, the MSA, or required by law
2. Use appropriate safeguards per 45 CFR § 164.314(a) and the Security Rule
3. Report Security Incidents and Breaches per Section 6
4. Ensure Subcontractors agree to same restrictions via written agreement
5. Make PHI available for individual access, amendment, and accounting per Covered Entity instructions
6. Make internal practices and records available to HHS for compliance determination
7. Return or destroy PHI at termination; if not feasible, extend protections
8. Not use PHI for marketing or sale prohibited by HIPAA

---

## 4. PERMITTED USES

FCA may use PHI to:

- Provide, maintain, and improve the Services for Covered Entity
- Conduct data aggregation for health care operations (if applicable and permitted)
- As required by law

---

## 5. OBLIGATIONS OF COVERED ENTITY

Covered Entity will:

- Provide notices of privacy practices and obtain authorizations where required
- Not request FCA to use PHI in violation of HIPAA
- Notify FCA of restrictions on PHI use/disclosure

---

## 6. BREACH NOTIFICATION

FCA will report Breach of Unsecured PHI to Covered Entity without unreasonable delay and within **sixty (60) days** maximum (or sooner per contractual SLA). Notification includes identification of affected individuals and PHI types to the extent known.

---

## 7. TERM AND TERMINATION

Term matches the MSA. Upon termination, FCA returns or destroys PHI per Section 3.7. Covered Entity may terminate if FCA violates material terms and cure is not possible.

---

## 8. MISCELLANEOUS

Governed by Commonwealth of Virginia and HIPAA. Conflict: HIPAA controls for PHI matters.
